"Word salad" refers to a string of words or phrases that is confused, nonsensical, and difficult to understand, both in the medical context of conditions like schizophrenia and in a more general sense for any incoherent or empty speech. In medicine, it's a symptom of thought disorder where words are jumbled into a meaningless collection. In broader usage, it describes any communication that lacks sense or substance, often characterized by a stream of vague or contradictory statements. 

The "word salad" aspect comes into play as the the user will choose words, sometimes nonsensically, sometimes trying to string together little bits of poetry or meaning, similar to how people who struggle with dysgraphia and schizophrenia have intent to speak, but it just doesn’t come out right. There’s beauty in the randomness of it all, especially in the randomness with intention. there’s chaos in a collage, and there’s chaos in our brains at all times. No collage of oneself is complete if it is truly organized, perhaps if only organized to the creator themselves.
